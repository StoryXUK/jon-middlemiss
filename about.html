Jonathan Middlemiss

Jonathan Middlemiss (1949 – 2021) was an internationally renowned artist known for his exceptional skills in creating intricate ceramic sculptures which integrate themes of geometry, ecology, and landscape. He described his ceramic forms and paintings as openings to different experiences of nature, using sensory immersion in the landscape to as the foundation of his work.

Jonathan explored maps of consciousness that offer portals to the physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual states that arise from engagement with nature. This metaphysical relationship used different visual languages to express different sensory experiences. He often used natural materials such as stone, wood and metal in his works.

The day before his passing he gifted all images and rights of his lifetime’s work to his daughter, with the instructions to use them and say “My Dad did that”. Jonathan always said that the art is his children’s legacy, Serena is committed to preserving this legacy with his life’s philosophy that inspired new perspectives in art and personal transformation.  With a background in design and a lifetime of understanding her father's work, Serena is the one person suited to organizing exhibitions, authenticating, and promoting her father's artwork. She currently cataloguing and documenting Jonathan’s extensive collection of art and writing his biography to ensure that his complete story is preserved and accessible for the future of art history.

His son Julian lives in Australia, he is an ardent explorer and family man, carrying his father’s essence and greatness in the lessons he's lived, learnt, taught and shared. Together they are working in collaboration to preserve his work for future generations. The combined knowledge of their father's work and life allow his legacy to continue in a meaningful way. They understands the themes, philosophies and techniques that Jonathan used, and have a keen eye for identifying his artwork.  

Jonathan Middlemiss was a talented artist who left behind a remarkable legacy and impressive collection of art that continues to inspire and captivate art enthusiasts worldwide. Middlemiss Art would very much like to hear from individual collectors, galleries and other people who had an interest in his life and work.
